## What is Data Science and what do Data Scientists do?

Over the course of this module, I developed a clear understanding of **what data science is and what data scientists actually do**. I learned that data science is an interdisciplinary field that combines programming, statistics, and domain knowledge to extract insights from data and support decision-making. Data scientists work with different types of data, apply algorithms, and use tools to clean, analyze, and interpret data. I also explored the variety of career paths within data science, the skills required (such as working with big data, file formats, and analytical techniques), and what a typical day in the life of a data scientist looks like. This helped me understand that data science is not just about coding, but about solving real-world problems and communicating insights effectively.

In addition, I gained foundational knowledge of **big data, machine learning, and data infrastructure**. I learned how big data drives digital transformation and how tools like Hadoop, Apache Spark, and cloud platforms support large-scale data processing. I was introduced to concepts such as machine learning, deep learning, artificial intelligence, and their real-world applications across industries. Furthermore, I explored how data is collected, stored, and managed using systems like relational databases and NoSQL, as well as concepts like data lakes, ETL pipelines, and metadata. Overall, this module showed me how data science is applied in different domains, the importance of data literacy, and the steps needed to build a career in this evolving field.
